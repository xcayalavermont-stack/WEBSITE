# Eliot P.S. Merrill — Professional Background Dossier for Arcadia Consulting

## TL;DR
- Eliot P.S. Merrill is a career private-equity investor with roughly 25 years of experience — 22 years at The Carlyle Group (2001–2025) and about 4 years earlier at Freeman Spogli & Co. — who rose to Managing Director and Co-Head (later Head) of Carlyle Global Partners, Carlyle's first long-dated buyout fund, and who now runs Arcadia Consulting as a strategic advisor to management teams, funds and boards.
- His deepest, most citable professional asset for a consulting bio is his extensive public- and private-company board governance experience (Getty Images, Nielsen, AMC Entertainment/Loews, TCW Group, NEP Group, TAMKO Building Products, Asplundh, Sciens Building Solutions, Hyundai Glovis, and Carlyle's publicly traded BDCs) combined with buyout deal leadership in media and telecom.
- Two claims require correction before publishing: Merrill did **not** serve on the board of BankUnited (that was Carlyle's separate Global Financial Services team), and his current firm is a new advisory practice he founded in 2024 — distinct from several unrelated companies that also use the name "Arcadia Consulting."

## Key Findings
1. **Carlyle tenure and seniority.** Merrill joined Carlyle in 2001, was promoted to Managing Director in the class announced January 2007, and remained an MD through 2025. He co-founded and co-headed Carlyle Global Partners (CGP), Carlyle's first long-dated private equity fund, and was later described as its sole Head.
2. **Investment focus.** For most of his career he focused on U.S. buyout opportunities in the telecommunications and media sectors; at CGP his mandate became sector- and geography-agnostic, oriented toward long-hold (10–15 year) investments.
3. **Board governance is his signature credential.** He has served on numerous corporate boards, several as a public-company director, including a stint as Interim Chairman of Carlyle's flagship business development company.
4. **Prior career.** Before Carlyle: Principal at Freeman Spogli & Co. (buyout fund); before that Dillon Read & Co. in M&A (1995–1997); and earlier a sail consultant/special-project coordinator at Doyle Sailmakers.
5. **Education.** A.B., magna cum laude, Harvard College (attended 1989–1993).
6. **Current activity.** Founder and CEO of Arcadia Consulting (since 2024), advising senior management teams; he also serves on the National Board of the Trust for Public Land.

## Details

### 1. Professional background at The Carlyle Group
Merrill spent 22 years at Carlyle (2001–2025). He was named a Managing Director in Carlyle's promotion class announced in January 2007, promoted from principal/director alongside peers such as Adam Palmer. For his first decade-plus he was, in Carlyle's own words, "a Managing Director focused on U.S. buyout opportunities in the telecommunications and media sectors," based in New York.

In 2014 Carlyle launched **Carlyle Global Partners (CGP)**, its first long-dated private equity fund, and Merrill became **Co-Head** alongside Tyler Zachem, who — per Carlyle's October 19, 2016 press release — "came to Carlyle in 2015 from Broad Sky Partners, where he was CEO and Founding Partner." Carlyle announced in that October 19, 2016 release ("The Carlyle Group Raises $3.6 Billion for First Long-Dated Private Equity Fund") that CGP had raised **$3.6 billion** and had "already invested or committed $1.1 billion of equity in four companies." It was one of the first PE vehicles designed to hold investments for more than 10 years; according to WSJ Pro Private Equity, "Carlyle indicated in 2016 that it expected the Global Partners fund to hold its typical investments for 15 years," versus an industry norm of "three to five years, with 10 years usually the outer limit." In a joint statement covered by Institutional Investor, co-heads Merrill and Zachem described CGP's "innovative" mandate to "invest long term alongside owners and management teams." The fund is "sector- and geography-agnostic, and has limited need for near[-term]" exits, and the CGP team at launch numbered 13 people across New York and London.

CGP's early portfolio (as of the 2016 fundraise announcement) comprised four deals totaling about $1.1 billion in equity: Global Jet Capital (aircraft finance), Content Partners (entertainment intellectual property), NEP Group (outsourced broadcast/live-event production), and Schoen Klinik (German specialty hospitals).

A **second fund, Carlyle Global Partners II (CGP II)**, was raised beginning around January 2019. Carlyle never issued an official "final close" announcement for CGP II. According to an SEC Form D reported by WSJ Pro Private Equity, "Carlyle Group has collected at least $1.84bn in commitments so far" for the fund, which it "has been raising… since at least January 2019," having "received commitments from at least 21 investors." PitchBook lists the fund at approximately $1.9 billion and "fully invested," with its last recorded investment (Sciens Building Solutions) in December 2021. In later Carlyle and Trust for Public Land bios, Merrill is described as sole **"Head of Carlyle Global Partners."**

**Notable deals/transactions he led or was closely involved in:**
- **Getty Images (2012):** Carlyle and Getty Images management acquired Getty Images from Hellman & Friedman for **$3.3 billion**; the equity came via Carlyle Partners V, Carlyle's $13.7 billion U.S. buyout fund. Merrill was the Carlyle Managing Director quoted on the deal — "Getty Images is the premier, digital global marketplace for commercial visual content. We look forward to partnering with Mark Getty, Jonathan Klein and the talented Getty Images management team. We will harness Carlyle's financial resources and global network to help take Getty Images to the next stage of product innovation and global growth" — and he joined the board.
- **AMC Entertainment / Loews Cineplex:** Merrill served as a director of AMC Entertainment (Parent and AMCE) from January 2008; he had earlier served on the board and audit committee of Loews Cineplex Entertainment Corporation (Carlyle was part of the AMC/Loews ownership group).
- **Nielsen:** He was a director of The Nielsen Company B.V. (Carlyle was part of the consortium that owned Nielsen); a Form 3 insider filing tied him to Nielsen Holdings N.V. around its 2011 IPO.
- **NEP Group (2016/2018):** CGP made a significant minority investment in June 2016 and acquired majority control (buying out Crestview Partners) in October 2018; Merrill has been an NEP director.
- **TAMKO Building Products (2019):** CGP became a long-term strategic minority investor in the asphalt-roofing manufacturer; Merrill, as co-head of CGP, was quoted on the deal and joined TAMKO's board.

**BankUnited — clarification.** Despite the assignment's hypothesis, there is no evidence Merrill ever served on BankUnited's board. SEC EDGAR full-text search for "Eliot Merrill" returns no BankUnited association; none of his biographies list BankUnited; and BankUnited's own board rosters do not include him. BankUnited was a Carlyle **Global Financial Services** team investment — part of the 2009 WL Ross / Carlyle / Blackstone / Centerbridge consortium recapitalization and the 2011 IPO — associated with Carlyle's John Redett (whose Carlyle bio explicitly lists BankUnited among his deals). That is a different team from Merrill's media/telecom and CGP practice. Separately, the older "BankUnited Financial Corp" (Florida, which failed in 2009) that appears in some SEC search results is an unrelated predecessor company and did not have Merrill on its board either.

## Recommendations
1. **Lead the bio with the board-governance and long-hold value-creation narrative.** This is the strongest, best-documented, and most differentiated asset for a consulting firm that advises management teams and boards. Use framing such as "25+ years as a private equity investor and public/private company director," and cite marquee names (Getty Images, Nielsen, AMC, TCW, NEP, TAMKO, Asplundh, Hyundai Glovis).
2. **Anchor Arcadia's positioning in his CGP experience** — patient, long-duration capital that "invests alongside owners and management teams." This maps naturally to advisory work on strategy, human capital, M&A, and ownership transitions (the exact themes on his own LinkedIn), and gives the firm a credible, ownable point of view distinct from generic strategy shops.
3. **Do not claim a BankUnited directorship.** It is inaccurate and easily disproven from SEC records; including it would undermine the firm's credibility.
4. **Verify the exact CGP II final size and his precise CGP title/date sequence directly with Merrill** before publishing hard numbers. Public sources firmly support $3.6 billion for CGP I but only third-party estimates (~$1.84–1.9 billion) for CGP II, and his title shifted from "Co-Head" to "Head" over time. Safest published phrasing: "co-founded and helped lead Carlyle's first long-dated private equity fund, which raised $3.6 billion."
5. **Disambiguate the company name.** Several unrelated firms use "Arcadia Consulting" — a UK behavioral-change consultancy (arcadiaconsulting.com), a New York forensic-accounting LLC (arcadiaconsultingnyc.com), a global business-advisory firm (arcadiaconsultinginc.com), and an insurance firm. Ensure Arcadia's website, domain, and copy clearly distinguish Merrill's practice (ideally with a unique tagline and consistent legal name) to avoid brand confusion and support search visibility.

**Benchmarks that would change these recommendations:** If Merrill provides primary documentation (offer letters, official Carlyle bios, fund private placement memoranda) confirming CGP II's final close or additional current board seats, upgrade those figures from "reported/estimated" to stated fact. If he confirms specific advisory engagements or target client sectors, foreground those as concrete proof points on the "About" and "Services" pages.
